Both directions presented in full with pros and cons. Toggle to compare.

PRG → KRK → WAW
  • ✅ Kraków on Wed–Fri — the three cleanest days. Every site at full hours, zero closures.
  • ✅ Prague Sat Jewish Quarter closure absorbed on arrival day when you'd be easing in anyway.
  • ✅ Warsaw's Tue closures avoided — Warsaw falls on Sat–Sun.
  • ✅ Late WAW departure (21:15) gives a full final day with time for the Rising Museum + Royal Route.
  • ⚠️ Long travel leg mid-trip (Prague→Kraków ~6h) when you may want to be sightseeing.
  • ⚠️ Ends in Warsaw — less scenic and celebratory as a trip finale than Prague.
WAW → KRK → PRG
  • ✅ Ends in Prague — the most compact, beautiful city makes for a lighter, celebratory finish.
  • ✅ Emotional arc — Poland's heavier history first, then ease into Prague's café culture and vibrant nightlife.
  • ✅ Short Pendolino first (Warsaw→Kraków ~2.5h), long leg at end when you're used to travel.
  • ⚠️ Kraków on Mon–Wed — Monday Wawel is limited hours (till 1pm) and Schindler's reduced (10–3).
  • ⚠️ Prague Sat Jewish Quarter closure falls on Day 8 (Sat May 30) when you'd want to be sightseeing.
  • ⚠️ Earlier PRG departure (19:30) — slightly less time on final day vs. WAW 21:15.
